About

Scientist. Engineer. Leader.

Starting from a B.Sc. in Electrical & Electronic Engineering at RUET, Bangladesh, I pursued a PhD at Florida Atlantic University specializing in Bioengineering — developing point-of-care viral diagnostics for Zika, Dengue, HIV, and COVID-19 that attracted $500K in NSF funding and media coverage across three TV networks.

Today at Beckman Coulter Diagnostics, I lead cross-functional teams in assay transfer, sensor optimization, and regulatory readiness for clinical chemistry platforms. I bring a rare combination of deep bench science, embedded quality ownership throughout the product lifecycle, and PMP®-certified project leadership.

My value to an organization sits at the intersection of IVD development, design quality & V&V, and scientific project management — equally effective driving a complex assay experiment, resolving a CAPA, or leading a milestone review with executive stakeholders.
